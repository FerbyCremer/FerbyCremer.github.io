About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science - Graphics and Visualization, at the University of Florida. I work in the SurfLab under the supervision of Dr. Jorg Peters. My current work focuses on human perception of complex 3D data sets (specifically medical imagry) in virtual reality, to both enhance spatial reasoning about the given structures and provide fast, yet accurate reconstructions without specialized software training.

When I'm not in the lab I can often be found either teaching CIS4930 - Design Patters or acting as Lead TA for COP4600 - Operating Systems under the direction of Dr. Jeremiah Blanchard, among other courses.

I graduated in Spring 2018 from the University of Florida with a Bachelor of Science degree in Digital Arts and Sciences with a personal focus on applied physics elevectives like Fluid Mechanics.
