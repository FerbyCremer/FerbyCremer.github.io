Vascular VR

Vascular VR is a soon-to-be cross-platform VR application meant bring state-of-the-art reconstruction technology to non-specialist physicians. By using stereoscopic rendering, natural navigation techniques, and proprioception Vascular VR allows clinicians to tweak and tune rapid prototype models created by Computer Vision Algorithms, without the need for time-consuming requests of uniquely trained engineers to perform the alterations.

While orginally invisioned as a stand-alone piece of software, SurfLab has since started the instantiation of an accompanying Python script to be used in Blender that will rig the blood vessel and tumor models with appropriate spring constraints. These rigged models are then used in another one of our lab's projects, TIPS, which is seen in proof-of-concept at the end of the embedded demo of the below presentation.

New features in the near future including a simple skinning of landmark anatomy point clouds to declutter the visual field while working. The current prototype code for this uses the basic Marching Cubes Algorithm.
