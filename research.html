DICOM-To-Mesh VR

This project is a cross-platform VR application meant to bring state-of-the-art reconstruction technology to non-specialist physicians. By using stereoscopic rendering, natural navigation techniques, and proprioception-enhanced memory - it allows clinicians to tweak and tune rapid prototype models created with the assitance of a segmentation algorithm module, without the need for time-consuming requests of uniquely trained engineers to perform the alterations.

While orginally invisioned as a stand-alone piece of software, SurfLab has since converted the software to work using the power of the Unity3D engine and OpenXR APIs. In addition to the central code we have also started the instantiation of an accompanying Python script, to be used in conjunction with Blender, to rig the blood vessel and tumor models with appropriate spring constraints. These rigged models are then used in another one of our lab's projects, TIPS, which is seen in proof-of-concept at the end of the embedded demo of the below presentation.
